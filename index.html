Severity: High

Early Blight (Alternaria solani)

AI Confidence Score: 94.8%

Symptoms Identified

Concentric target-like rings with yellow halos starting on older leaves. Rapid defoliation occurs on lower portions of the plant first.

Root Causes

Fungal spores that thrive in wet weather, high humidity, and warm temperatures (24°C - 29°C). Transmitted by wind and rain splash.

Recommended Treatment Plan

Organic / Cultural Control
  • Prune infected lower foliage immediately and burn/destroy it (do not compost).
  • Apply neem oil (10ml/L of water) to slow leaf spread.
  • Water only at the soil level to keep leaves dry. Multi-crop rotation next season.
Chemical Control
  • Spray Copper-based fungicides (like Bordeaux mixture) at 7-10 day intervals.
  • For severe spreads, apply Chlorothalonil or Mancozeb as directed on package labels.
  • Observe a safety pre-harvest waiting interval of 7 days post spraying.
